
# How to Record and Upload a Database Process Video (Windows)

## 1. Prepare Your Presentation Slides as a Teleprompter

1. Create your step-by-step instructions as slides in Microsoft PowerPoint.
2. Use concise bullet points for each step you want to narrate.
3. Arrange the slides in the order you will present them.
4. Open your PowerPoint in "Slide Show" mode or "Reading View" to use as a teleprompter while recording.

## 2. Record Your Screen and Voice with PowerPoint

1. Open your PowerPoint presentation.
2. Go to the "Slide Show" tab and select "Record Slide Show" to record narration and slide transitions, **or**
3. Go to the "Insert" tab, then click "Screen Recording" (far right) to record your screen and voice as you demonstrate steps (e.g., switching between slides and your database process).
4. Select the area of your screen to record (include both your slides and any application windows you want to show).
5. Click the "Record" button. Use your slides as a teleprompter and narrate your process as you work.
6. To pause, click the "Pause" button on the small control bar at the top of the screen. Click "Record" again to resume.
7. Click "Stop" when finished.
8. The recording appears in your slide. Right-click the video and select "Save Media as..." to export the video file.

## 3. Edit or Combine Segments (Optional)

- Open Start menu, type "Video Editor," and launch the app.
- Create a new project and add your video file(s).
- Trim, split, or combine as needed.
- Click "Finish video" to export the final version.

## 4. Upload to YouTube as Unlisted

1. Go to https://www.youtube.com and sign in.
2. Click the camera icon (top right) and select "Upload video."
3. Select your exported video file.
4. Enter a title and description.
5. Under "Visibility," select "Unlisted."
6. Click "Save" or "Publish."

## 5. Get the Unlisted Video URL

1. After upload, YouTube will show a link to your video.
2. Copy the URL (e.g., https://youtu.be/xxxxxxx).
3. Submit this URL as required.

---

Tip: Using your slides as a teleprompter helps you stay on script and ensures a smooth, clear narration during your screen recording.

If you need help with any step, ask for more details.
